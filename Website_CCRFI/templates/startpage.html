Corporate credit rating feature importance: Does ESG matter?

Corportate credit rating predictor

Background research

The prediction model for corporate credit ratings stems from research investigated in Corporate credit rating feature importance: Does ESG matter?

ESG features are not required to obtain a rating in this instance. Only quarterly accounting based features are needed as inputs. The trained model is >91% accurate on test data.

As green bond issuance and sustainability-linked loans can attract increased media exposure, in addition to impact investors, large, and smaller institutional investors holding these to satisfy their investment mandates, investigating which ESG features are drivers of corporate credit predictability has important real world implications in capital markets functioning (e.g., finding mispriced bonds).

We introduce ESG features into US and global firm samples with accounting, microeconomic, and macroeconomic features exhibited in the literature for corporate credit rating prediction.

In terms of classification models, sophisticated models (e.g., support vector machines, neural networks, and tree-based ensembles) are compared to more traditional models (e.g., logistic regression and quadratic discriminant analysis) for predictive ability.

Advocated by López de Prado (2020), mean decrease impurity (MDI) and mean decrease accuracy (MDA) introduced by Breiman (2001), and Tree SHapley Additive exPlanations (TreeSHAP) introduced by Lundberg et al. (2018) and Lundberg and Lee (2017) are utilised for feature selection.

Key findings of paper

Evaluates the performance of x14 statistical learning models using approx. 480 features of accounting, macroeconomic, microeconomic, and ESG on a US and global dataset over the time frame of 1982-2019.

(i) Random forests and extremely randomised trees models, with MDI, MDA and TreeSHAP feature importance methods, provide > 91% multi-class corporate credit rating prediction accuracy.

(ii) ESG features are more important for investment-grade classes, whilst financial ratios exhibit greater importance for speculative-grade classes.

(iii) For US and global firms, Thomson Reuters Diversity and Inclusion Rating metrics of diversity score, inclusion score, people development score, and score are highly important corporate credit rating features.

(iv) The social factors comprising the aforementioned metrics are consistent with the findings presented in Attig et al. (2013). Furthermore, the high importance of institutional owners in the presence of ESG features for US firms resembles Tang and Zhang (2020) findings that green bond issuance increases institutional ownership.

(v) MDI and MDA feature importance methods identifies features that provide greater out-of-sample multi-class prediction, however TreeSHAP provides insights into which features are driving the prediction of per class corporate credit ratings.
